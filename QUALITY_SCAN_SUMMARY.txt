4. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
   
   Why is 0.40 producing so many false positives?
   
   a) Splink TF-IDF Problem:
      - Common Irish surnames (Wray, Gillespie, Bustard) are downweighted
      - This forces algorithm to rely on weaker signals (first name, age)
      - Multiple people with same surname+first name exist in small townland
      - Result: Ambiguous matches at 0.40 score
   
   b) Age Estimation Error Amplification:
      - Census ages had ±2-3 year errors at most
      - 0.40 linkages show 10-40 year discrepancies
      - These indicate different people, not estimation error
   
   c) Small Population Problem:
      - Tullynaught has only ~3,167 persons across 3 censuses
      - High probability of duplicate name combinations
      - Splink matches these indiscriminately at loose thresholds
